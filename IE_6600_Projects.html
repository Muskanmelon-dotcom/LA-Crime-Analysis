An Introduction : Crimes in the City of Los Angeles

This dataset represents reported incidents of crime in the City of Los Angeles dating back to 2020. The information is transcribed from original paper-based crime reports, so there may be occasional inaccuracies. Some location fields may be marked as (0°, 0°) indicating missing data. Address details are limited to the nearest hundred block to protect privacy. The accuracy of this data is contingent on the information available in the database. To ensure that there is proper representation of data across the years, the dataset is filtered from start of January 2020 till December 2022 and data from 2023 is removed from the analysis.

Data Source : https://data.lacity.org/Public-Safety/Crime-Data-from-2020-to-Present/2nrs-mtv8

Executive Summary

From the subsequent analysis the following insights were found :

  • Crime has increased from 2020-2022 in Los Angeles.

  • Crime is significantly more per 1000 residents in Los Angeles than the national average.

  • Central L.A. (LAPD Patrol Area) has become the epicenter of crime over the years.

  • Stolen Vehicle (Grand Theft Auto) is the most frequent crime in the city of Los Angeles.

  • A disproportionately high spike in Identity Theft has been observed from 2021 to 2022.

  • Fridays and Saturdays have above average crimes - with Fridays being the day with most number of incidents of crime.

  • The majority of victims in each year are Male identifying.

  • There is a marked increase in the number of Female victims from 2021 to 2022.

  • Most number of victims are in the age bracket of 30-40 i.e. they are/were in their 30s.

  • Incidents of crime peak around the Afternoon hours (12:01pm - 6:00pm).

  • Most incidents of crime get reported within a day, however there is another peak of reporting within 7 days of the occurence of incident.

Analyzing Crime

Total Incidents of Crime over the years in L.A.

There is an increase in the crime incidents over the years in the City of Los Angeles. In the year 2020 the number of incidents are - 199,409 while in the subsequent two years 2021 and 2022 it is - 209,353 & 234,227. The increase year on year is 5% for 2020 - 2021 while it is almost 12% for 2021-2022.

Crime Incidents Adjusted by Population

The Crime Rate in Los Angeles is compared below w.r.t the national Average (per 1000 residents) . It should be noted that it is significantly more than the national average.

Hotbeds of Crime in L.A.

Over the years crime has increased in the city of Los Angeles. The hotbeds of crime by LAPD patrol areas are - 

  • 77th Street

  • Pacific

  • Southwest

  • Southeast

  • Central

Over the years Central L.A. has become the epicenter of crime in L.A. and in 2022 it was the leading LAPD patrol area for incidents of crime. The 'Year of Incidents' selection filter at the top provides the option to track number of incidents of crime by LAPD patrol area in the selected year.

In the next section a Tree-map is shown to perform a drill down analysis of the types of crime prevalent in L.A.

Below, is a tree map depicting the types of crime in various LAPD patrol areas over the years (2020-2022). At a glance one can note that the major type of crime across patrol areas is - Vehicle Stolen i.e Grand Theft Auto.

Frequent Crimes in L.A.

In the next visualization we list the most frequently occuring crimes in L.A filtering by the year. Corroborating with our earlier insight - Vehicle Stolen i.e Grand Theft Auto is the most frequent crime over the years.

An interesting point to be noted here is the huge increase in Theft of Identity in the year 2022 which we will explore in the next visualization.

As is evident from the previous visualization - there is a huge spike in Identity Theft from 2021 to 2022. We present the three major crimes overall with the help of Split Bars. There is a huge spike of almost 100% in Identity Theft from 2021 to 2022.

Total Incidents of Crime by Day of Week

On analyzing the incidents of crime by the day of the week in Los Angeles - we come across an interesting finding - the maximum incidents of crime occur on Fridays - closely followed by the Saturday. Both of these days have above average crime compared to other days of the week.

On diving deeper - we see that 2020 had 52 Fridays, 2021 had 53 Fridays and 2022 had 52 Fridays. To make sure that we were not over-reporting the number of incidences of crime due to the extra Friday in 2021, we broke down the incident aggregations by year in the next analysis.

Total Incidents of Crime by Day of Week - Analysis over the Years

As stated above - the year 2021 has 53 Fridays while 2020 and 2022 have 52 Fridays, so we confirm that the number of incidents per day of the week are not over-reported for Friday as we have done a year wise analysis. Our analysis concludes that Friday's have the most number of incidents of crime in L.A. region in each year.

Who is the Victim ?

Victims by Sex

On analysis of the profile of the victims of crime based on sex the following insights came up -

  • The majority of victims over the years were Male identifying.

  • There was a marked increase in the number of victims who were female in 2022 (16%) compared to the overall increase in number of victims (13%).

A point to be noted here is - when performing aggregations based on Victim Sex - some nulls were encountered, being less than 13% of the dataset - the nulls were dropped without loss of generality.

Victims by Age

On analyzing the age of victims the following histogram is derived.

  • The age of victims is binned into 10 year bins with the minimum victim age being 0 and maximum being 99 (from dataset).

  • The majority of victims are in age bracket of 30-40 i.e. they are in their 30s.

  • The second major age group of victims is in the age bracket of 20-30 i.e. they are in their 20s.

Diving Deeper into Crime in L.A.

Crime by Time of Day

The dataset contains the time at which the incident of crime occurred. The time was binned into 4 segments - Morning, Afternoon, Evening and Night. The binning was done in the following manner - 

  • Morning (6:01am - 12:00pm)

  • Afternoon (12:01pm - 6:00pm)

  • Evening (6:01pm - 12:00am)

  • Night (12:01am - 6:00am)

The incidents of crime have it's peak during the Afternoon hours (12:01pm - 6:00pm).

How long does it take for Crime to be Reported ?

Majority of crimes get reported within 1 Day of occurrence of the incident of crime. What is noted here is that there is a spike in the number of crimes which are reported within 7 Days of occurrence.
